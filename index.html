The Story Behind the Exhibit

Sri was a very active and joyful child in her toddler years. The exhibit displays this phase of her life in the ground floor section of the Doll house, all playful and joyous.

But then a Type 1 Diabetes (T1D) diagnosis brought sudden challenges that turned her world upside down. Through the overwhelming changes, the dolls in her life became symbols of comfort, stability, and friendship. This is depicted in the middle section of the doll house exhibit where the darkness represents the heaviness of diagnosis. Each doll stood by her during the toughest moments—offering a space for imagination, expression, and courage. They helped her navigate fear and uncertainty, ultimately growing into a confident advocate.

Today, as depicted on the 3rd floow of the doll house exhibit, Sri shares her story on platforms such as TEDx, BreakthroughT1D stages, and AnimaT1D’s YouTube channel, inspiring others through creativity, resilience, and her vision for a better future with T1D.

The topmost floor of the Doll house exhibit shows all the characters standing strong, looking forward to a cure, inspiring others through creativity, resilience, and her vision for a better future without T1D.
